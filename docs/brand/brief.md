# Zonot — Brand Brief

*A calm, clear surface over a deep, connected store. A notes app named for the deep water you reach through a small opening in the ground.*

**Pronunciation:** ZOH-not · **Category:** notes / personal knowledge app · **Guiding rule:** *evoke the imagery, don’t invoke the sources.*

-----

## The core idea

A cenote is a small, calm opening in the ground that drops into a vast, crystal-clear, interconnected underwater world. That is what Zonot is for a notes app: the surface — capture — is simple, quiet, and uncluttered, while underneath, everything you keep connects into one deep, navigable system.

- **Calm on top, deep underneath.** Dropping a thought in is effortless; the richness lives below.
- **Everything connects.** Cenotes link into vast flooded cave networks — the model for linked notes and a knowledge base that joins up rather than piling up.
- **Clarity.** The water is famously clear; you can see all the way down. Finding what you kept should feel the same.

This is the reason the name fits — not just that it sounds good.

## Positioning (one line)

A clear place to drop your thinking, where everything you keep stays connected below the surface.

## Visual direction

- **Palette:** deep, clear blues and teals; bright water, not murky depths.
- **Motifs:** a single shaft of light falling through still water; the threshold where ground opens into depth; the sense of seeing to the bottom.
- **Feel:** bright and aquatic, calm and spacious — a sanctuary, not a dashboard.
- **Avoid:** Maya glyphs or iconography, “sacred well” mysticism, fantasy/gaming cues. Anything that *names* a source instead of evoking the image.

## Voice & tone

Calm, clear, and deep. Plainspoken, with a sense of depth underneath — *“a clear place to think,”* never *“ancient secrets of the well.”* Confident and quiet. Let the water do the talking.

## Tagline shortlist

- Calm on the surface. Deep underneath.
- A clear place for everything you think.
- Drop it in. Find it forever.
- Where your thinking collects.
- Still water, deep roots.

## Provenance (handle quietly)

Keep the origin as a footnote, never a headline. No origin story on the homepage, no iconography in the UI. At most, one understated line in a footer or deep on an About page (e.g., *“named for the deep, clear freshwater wells”*).

*For internal accuracy:* Zonot is adapted from the Yucatec Maya **dzonot / ts’ono’ot** — a water-filled sinkhole or well with accessible groundwater, and the root from which Spanish “cenote” derives. If the origin is ever stated publicly, frame it as *“adapted from,”* credit Yucatec Maya, and don’t claim it as the literal spelling.
